About Animal Crossing

Animal Crossing is a series of life simulation video games developed and published by Nintendo, in which the player lives in a village inhabited by anthropomorphic animals, carrying out various activities such as fishing, bug catching, and fossil hunting. The series was created by Katsuya Eguchi and Hisashi Nogami, and is directed by Aya Kyogoku. The series has been released on multiple platforms, including the GameCube, Nintendo DS, Wii, Nintendo 3DS, Wii U, Nintendo Switch, and mobile phones. The series has sold over 80 million copies worldwide, making it one of the best-selling video game franchises of all time. The series has received critical acclaim, with critics praising the game's relaxing gameplay and its ability to provide a sense of community. The series has also received several awards, including the 2003 Game Developers Choice Award for Best Handheld Game, the 2006 Game Developers Choice Award for Best Console Game, and the 2013 Game Developers Choice Award for Best Handheld/Mobile Game.
